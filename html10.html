Folk art is a form of art created by people who are not formally trained in the arts. It is often seen as a reflection of the culture and beliefs of a particular community. Folk art typically includes paintings, sculptures, textiles, pottery, and other decorative arts.

Common subjects of folk art include animals, plants, landscapes, and religious symbols. Folk art often has a distinct style, with bright colors, intricate patterns, and symbolic imagery. Folk art is typically passed down from generation to generation, and can be found in most cultures around the world.
